Malala Yousafzai

July-12-1997

Malala Yousafzai, (born July 12, 1997, Mingora, Swat valley, Pakistan), Pakistani activist who, while a teenager, spoke out publicly against the prohibition on the education of girls that was imposed by the Tehrik-e-Taliban Pakistan (TTP; sometimes called Pakistani Taliban). She gained global attention when she survived an assassination attempt at age 15. Biographics

  • Birth and Early Life: Malala Yousafzai was born on July 12, 1997, in Mingora, Pakistan, in the Swat Valley, to Ziauddin Yousafzai, an education activist, and Tor Pekai Yousafzai, a homemaker.
  • Education Advocacy: Malala became an advocate for girls' education in Pakistan, speaking out against the Taliban's ban on girls attending school in the Swat Valley.
  • Writing for BBC: At the age of 11, Malala started writing a blog for the BBC Urdu service under a pseudonym, detailing her life under the Taliban's rule and advocating for education.
  • Taliban Attack: In 2012, Malala was shot in the head by a Taliban gunman while on a bus ride home from school. The attack was an assassination attempt by the Taliban due to her activism.
  • Survival and Recovery: Malala survived the attack and was flown to the United Kingdom for treatment. She underwent surgeries and extensive rehabilitation.
  • Global Recognition: Malala's story gained international attention, and she became a symbol of resilience and the fight for girls' education worldwide.
  • Nobel Peace Prize: In 2014, Malala became the youngest-ever Nobel Peace Prize laureate at the age of 17, sharing the prize with Kailash Satyarthi, an Indian children's rights activist.
  • Malala Fund: Malala co-founded the Malala Fund with her father, aiming to ensure every girl receives 12 years of free, safe, and quality education.
  • Advocacy and Speeches: She continued her activism, giving speeches at the United Nations and various platforms to advocate for education and women's rights.
  • Book and Documentary: Malala wrote an autobiography titled "I Am Malala: The Girl Who Stood Up for Education and Was Shot by the Taliban." Her story was also documented in films and documentaries.
  • Education Pursuits: Despite the attack, Malala pursued her education, attending the University of Oxford, where she studied Philosophy, Politics, and Economics.
  • Global Impact: She remains an influential figure globally, advocating for education and women's rights through her speeches, interviews, and various initiatives.
  • Recognition and Awards: Malala has received numerous awards and honors for her activism, including honorary Canadian citizenship and an honorary doctorate from the University of King's College.
  • Activism Beyond Education: Malala's activism extends beyond education, advocating for refugees, women's empowerment, and social equality on a global scale.
  • Personal Life and Future: Malala continues her advocacy work while balancing her personal life, aiming to inspire others and bring about positive change through education and activism.
